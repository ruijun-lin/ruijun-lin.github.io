About Me

I am a PhD student in the School of Mathematics and Physics at the University of Wollongong (UoW), Australia. My supervisor is Professor Aidan Sims (Honorary).

My master’s degree was awarded at the University of Copenhagen. My master’s thesis supervisor is Professor Søren Eilers, who is mainly working on operator algeras and related, especially on graph C*-algebras.

My bachelor’s degree was awarded at Southern University of Science and Technology, Shenzhen China. My undergraduate academic advisor was Yifei Zhu, whose research interests are algebraic topology and related fields.
My undergraduate thesis supervisor was Bochen Liu , whose research interests are harmonic analysis, geometric measure theory and combinatorics.

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of operator algebras, combinatorics, and topology. Specifically, I am interested in:

  • k-graphs (higher-rank graphs): Generalisations of directed graphs that encode multi-dimensional structure. They provide a rich source of examples of C*-algebras and serve as a testing ground for structural theorems.

  • Groupoids: I study étale groupoids arising from k-graphs and other combinatorial objects. The groupoid model is a powerful tool for understanding the K-theory, ideal structure, and classification of the associated C*-algebras.

  • Operator Algebras: Broadly speaking, I am interested in C*-algebras and their invariants, including K-theory, traces, and ideal structures.
